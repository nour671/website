What is Optical Character Recognition (OCR) ?

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is a process that converts an image of text into a machine-readable text format. For example, if you scan a form or a receipt, your computer will save the scan as an image file.
You cannot use a text editor to edit, search, or count the words in an image file. However, you can use Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to convert an image into a text document and store its contents as text data.
